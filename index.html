    üé§ Interview Frameworks & Questions

    Behavioral Questions (STAR Framework)

    STAR Method

    • Situation ‚Äî Set the context briefly (company, scale, challenge)
    • Task ‚Äî Your specific responsibility and goal
    • Action ‚Äî What YOU did (specific, technical, leadership-oriented)
    • Result ‚Äî Quantified outcome ($ saved, % improved, scale achieved)

    Common VP-Level Behavioral Questions

    • "Tell me about a time you built an AI team from scratch."
    • "Describe a failed AI project. What went wrong and what did you learn?"
    • "How have you convinced skeptical executives to invest in AI?"
    • "Tell me about navigating a significant organizational change."
    • "Describe your approach to managing underperformers on your team."
    • "How do you prioritize competing AI initiatives with limited resources?"
    • "Tell me about a time you had to pivot an AI strategy mid-execution."

    Response Tips

    • Keep Situation/Task to 20% of the answer, Action/Result to 80%
    • Always quantify results: "Reduced processing time by 73%, saving $4.2M annually"
    • Show strategic thinking, not just execution
    • Demonstrate learning from failures ‚Äî vulnerability builds trust at VP level

    Strategic Scenario Questions

    Common Scenarios

    Scenario 1: "Our CEO just declared we need to be an AI-first company. Where do you start?"
    • Clarify what "AI-first" means ‚Äî augmenting existing or creating new?
    • Assess current maturity: data, talent, infrastructure, governance
    • Identify 3-5 highest-impact use cases aligned to strategic priorities
    • Build the 30-60-90 plan with quick wins + long-term vision
    • Establish success metrics and governance framework
    Scenario 2: "We've spent $50M on AI with limited ROI. How do you fix this?"
    • Audit the current portfolio: which initiatives are delivering, which aren't
    • Diagnose root causes: data quality? wrong problems? no productionization? culture?
    • Kill underperforming projects decisively ‚Äî sunk cost discipline
    • Redirect investment to highest-ROI, most production-ready initiatives
    • Implement rigorous stage-gate process for future investments
    Scenario 3: "Our competitor just launched an AI product that threatens our market share."
    • Assess the competitive threat objectively ‚Äî differentiation vs. feature parity
    • Identify our unique data assets and domain expertise (competitive moat)
    • Fast-follow on table-stakes features, differentiate on proprietary capabilities
    • Accelerate go-to-market through partnerships or acquisitions if needed
    • Turn defense into offense: what AI advantage can we build that they can't replicate?

    AI Cost Optimization

    Cost Levers

    • Model Optimization ‚Äî Quantization (INT8/INT4), distillation, pruning
    • Infrastructure ‚Äî Spot instances, reserved capacity, right-sizing GPU selection
    • Inference ‚Äî Batching, caching, model routing (small ‚Üí large), speculative decoding
    • Training ‚Äî Mixed precision, gradient checkpointing, efficient architectures
    • Data ‚Äî Active learning (label less data more effectively), data augmentation
    • Architecture ‚Äî Cascade models, embeddings cache, async processing

    LLM Cost Benchmarks

    • GPT-4 class: $15-30/1M tokens (input), $30-60/1M tokens (output)
    • GPT-3.5 class: $0.50-1/1M tokens
    • Self-hosted Llama 70B: ~$2-4/hr (A100 GPU), amortized per token much lower at scale
    • Break-even: self-hosting typically wins at >100K requests/day

    Key Phrases

    AI Security & Privacy

    AI Security Threats

    • Prompt Injection ‚Äî Direct/indirect injection attacks on LLMs
    • Data Poisoning ‚Äî Adversarial training data manipulation
    • Model Extraction ‚Äî Stealing model weights through API queries
    • Membership Inference ‚Äî Determining if data was in training set
    • PII Leakage ‚Äî Models memorizing and regurgitating sensitive data

    Defense Strategies

    • Input/output sanitization and validation layers
    • PII detection and redaction pipelines
    • Rate limiting and anomaly detection on model APIs
    • Differential privacy in training
    • Regular red-teaming and adversarial testing
    • Data access controls and audit logging

    Key Phrases

    • "AI security is the next frontier of cybersecurity. Most enterprises haven't even started thinking about it."
    • "Every LLM deployment needs an input/output security layer ‚Äî prompt injection is the SQL injection of AI."
    • "Red-teaming AI systems should be as routine as penetration testing web applications."
