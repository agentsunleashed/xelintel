AI CoE Structure

  • Hub-and-spoke: Central platform team + embedded domain specialists in BUs
  • Central team owns: platform, standards, governance, talent development
  • Spokes own: domain-specific use cases, business integration, adoption
  • CoE is a force multiplier, not a bottleneck

Central vs Federated

  • Centralized: Full control, slower velocity — works early stage
  • Federated: Fast but inconsistent — works with mature teams
  • Hub-and-Spoke: Best at scale — reusable capabilities + domain speed
  • Governance is always centrally defined, locally enforced

AI Governance Structure

  • Governance is a prerequisite for scale, not a tax on innovation
  • Risk-tiered model: lightweight for low-risk, rigorous for high-stakes
  • Clear ownership: every model has an accountable business owner
  • AI Review Board: cross-functional, meets bi-weekly, decisions documented
  • Governance framework: approve, monitor, audit, decommission

Responsible AI & Ethics

  • Fairness: Bias detection, equitable outcomes, demographic parity testing
  • Transparency: Explainability (SHAP, LIME), model cards, audit trails
  • Safety: Red-teaming, guardrails, human-in-the-loop for high-stakes decisions
  • Privacy: Differential privacy, data minimization, consent management
  • Accountability: Clear ownership, incident response, remediation protocols

Security, Compliance, Risk

  • Model risk management: Validation, stress testing, drift monitoring
  • Regulatory alignment: EU AI Act, NIST AI RMF, industry-specific compliance
  • Proactive compliance — build for regulation before it arrives
